About Me

Work

Currently, I work as a fullstack[1] web developer. More specifically, I spend (approximately) 75% of my time writing Python microservices for deployment in a Linux environment in AWS, and the other 25% creating single page web applications in Angular. I also perform various devops-related tasks on an ad-hoc basis. That said, this ratio varies depending on the needs of my team and the project.

Home

While I love web development, both at work and in my free time, I also engage in much wider variety of coding and other technical pursuits. These include

  • Linux system programming in C
  • Infosec research
  • Building, modifying, and pen-testing my home lab network
  • Maintaing and tweaking my Arch Linux installations
  • Creating and contributing to various open source projectcs

Although the above list may seem somewhat eclectic, I can attribute all of my technical predilictions - from my fondness for low-level, close-to-the-metal languages like C to my passion for coding in general - to my need to truly understand how things work.

Programming Languages

Why Blog?

When learning new technologies I invariably find myself taking notes and, prior to publishing this blog, these notes were disparate, disorganized, and lacking in any structural/stylistic consistency. As such, in addition to serving as a portfolio - and occasionally as a soapbox - this blog primarily functions as my digital notebook.
